**Box scores and features**

**ARENA DISTRICT.** `06_sports` supplies athletic department clichés without attaching them to real franchises. Final scores, injury reports, and trade rumors are omitted or generic so the file stays legally inert while still feeling like a sports page.

Columnists argued about clutch performance versus season-long metrics. Analytics departments favor expected goals, launch angles, and pace-adjusted ratings; traditionalists favor intangibles. This paragraph exists to mix numerals, slashes, and hyphens inside narrow measure.

High-school coverage noted postseason brackets and travel budgets. Booster clubs and eligibility rules get a nod. Youth sports copy often includes long organization names; watch overfull hboxes when you insert them without hyphenation hints.

Outdoor editors previewed a trail race series with elevation gain described qualitatively as “stiff climbs.” Weather contingencies and medical tents appear as checklist items. Photos would show mud and bib numbers; the template supplies figure slots elsewhere.

Olympic-year writers stockpiled evergreen explainers on qualification paths. National committees, federation spats, and doping protocol vocabulary raise token diversity for spellcheck. None of it implies live results.

Fantasy analysts listed start-sit heuristics without naming players protected by publicity rights. Replace with public-domain rosters if you publish externally. The build system cares only that LaTeX special characters remain escaped inside user strings.

Sports business reporters tied media rights to cord-cutting trends. Streaming exclusives and blackout rules echo the technology desk’s themes. Cross-department duplication is acceptable in newspapers; in repositories, prefer linking to one canonical doc.

The desk signs off with a reminder that late games may miss print deadlines. Pipeline runs should schedule PDF generation after known content freezes. Sports completes; arts and culture turn next.

**Depth notes.** Salary caps and luxury taxes create strategic roster puzzles general managers solve under uncertainty. Fans debate max contracts; analysts build Monte Carlo playoff simulators. This markdown includes no Monte Carlo code—only narrative rhythm.

Injury reports list practice participation levels with opaque wording—questionable, doubtful, probable—until leagues standardize further. Medical privacy laws constrain detail. Sports editors balance reader demand with HIPAA-adjacent caution.

Officiating controversies spawn slow-motion clips and rulebook appendices. Replay centers add latency to games but reduce obvious errors. Print deadlines still hit whether or not overtime resolves.

College eligibility rules for name-image-likeness payments evolve quickly. Compliance offices train athletes on disclosure. Student newspapers cover campus teams with different legal constraints than national wires.

Youth concussion protocols now bench players after suspected head impacts. Return-to-play steps are medical, not editorial. Template authors writing real youth coverage should link to governing-body policies.

Esports leagues negotiate franchise slots and patch versions mid-season. Traditional sports desks sometimes sneer; readers under thirty do not. Cross-platform storytelling might mix Twitch clips with print recaps—out of scope here.

Venue financing mixes public bonds with team equity. Taxpayers debate opportunity costs. Business desks (`04_business.md`) carry dollars; sports carries drama. Collaboration beats duplication.

Sports final: `06_sports` ends on schedule so `07_arts` can open with slower, more descriptive cadence.
