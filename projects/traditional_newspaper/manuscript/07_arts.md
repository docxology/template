**Depth notes.** Copyright terms differ by jurisdiction and genre; fair use analyses weigh transformative purpose and market harm. Arts critics quote sparingly; lawyers read every line. This template avoids long quotations to reduce rights review overhead for maintainers.

Digital restoration of films raises grain-versus-noise debates. Color timing choices privilege director intent or historical accuracy depending on who funds the scan. Archivists document provenance; PDF authors should document font embedding decisions the same way.

Public art permits involve city councils, transit authorities, and sometimes federal percent-for-art programs. Sculptures need engineering stamps; murals need anti-graffiti coatings. None of that appears visually here—only prose placeholders.

Choreography copyright remains unsettled in some courts; TikTok dances complicate ownership narratives. Dance critics describe movement without reproducing it verbatim—similar to how we describe pipelines without pasting entire log files into markdown.

Literary prizes influence translation deals and bookstore placement. Shortlists leak; publishers embargo. Static markdown cannot leak; it only ages when committees change names.

Sound design in podcasts uses room tone, compression, and music beds under speech. Transcripts aid accessibility. Web exports from this project could attach transcripts later if infrastructure adds hooks.

Museum decolonization debates ask who tells whose story. Curatorial practice shifts toward community co-creation. Exemplar text nods respectfully without simulating trauma narratives.

Arts finale: `07_arts` values cadence over citations. The unsigned editorial on `08_editorial.md` switches voice to institutional first-person plural.
