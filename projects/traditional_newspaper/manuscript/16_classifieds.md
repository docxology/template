**Brief lines**

**CLASSIFIEDS.** `16_classifieds` is the last core stem in `PAGE_SLICES`. Short items sell used furniture, offer tutoring, and seek roommates without phone numbers. Python helper `classified_line` in `snippets.py` formats bold leads with optional italic detail; this file mostly uses Markdown bold for the same visual rhythm.

Garage sales list hours as “Saturday early.” Rain dates implied. Photo attachments would be classified display ads in revenue models; not modeled here.

Help-wanted stubs sought baristas and junior analysts. Wage ranges omitted. Labor law compliance is on publishers, not on this template.

Lost-and-found mentioned a scarf at a transit stop, color unspecified. Return-to-owner narratives are heartwarming filler.

Community calendars referenced open mics and watershed cleanups. ISO dates avoided to reduce churn.

**Depth notes.** Classified advertising once funded newsrooms substantially; digital marketplaces ate that lunch. Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, and vertical apps displaced print lines. Studying layout here is partly historical curiosity.

Legal notices—foreclosures, name changes, fictitious business statements—carry statutory text. They pay by the line; narrow measure saves money. `multicols` compression interacts with pricing metaphors amusingly.

Personals sections shrank as dating apps grew. Discrimination in housing and employment ads prompted legal reforms. Automated moderation still struggles with coded language.

Automotive classifieds need VIN disclosure in some jurisdictions. Odometer fraud laws bite sellers. Real listings demand diligence; fake listings train readers to distrust.

Pet ads should discourage puppy mills; shelters promote adoption. Animal welfare copy belongs in ethics policies.

Employment law forbids certain discriminatory qualifiers in help-wanted ads. HR departments vet language; this template uses neutral stubs.

Classifieds coda: stem `16_classifieds` is the last mandatory file `validate_inventory` checks. Anything after it in the PDF is supplemental by design.
