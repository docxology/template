**Public health and clinics**

**HEALTH.** Stem `13_health` treats medical topics generically. It is not clinical advice. Sentences discuss vaccination scheduling metaphors, clinic wait times, and mental-health access without dosing instructions or diagnoses.

Epidemiologists-in-prose noted seasonality of respiratory viruses. Hand hygiene and staying home when febrile remain evergreen tips. Data dashboards cited elsewhere are absent; the template does not pull CDC APIs during render.

Pediatricians emphasized sleep and screen-time boundaries for adolescents. Parenting columns thrive on empathy; this stub supplies empathy without case studies.

Rural health correspondents described travel distances to specialists. Telehealth bridges gaps when broadband cooperates. Infrastructure parallels `07_arts` festival connectivity themes lightly.

Nutrition writers avoided fad diet names. Whole grains, legumes, and vegetables appear as a bland consensus list. Allergen warnings would need precision if you ship externally.

Mental-health advocates promoted crisis hotlines as strings of digits in a real paper; here they are described obliquely to avoid maintaining phone numbers in git. Use config-driven injection for live numbers.

Insurance literacy paragraphs defined deductibles versus out-of-pocket caps in plain language. Financial toxicity in cancer care got a serious clause because column tone should vary.

**Depth notes.** Clinical trials register protocols before enrollment to reduce selective reporting. CONSORT checklists standardize tables. Health journalists should link to trial IDs when summarizing results—this text omits IDs.

Pharmacovigilance systems collect adverse events post-market. Signal detection uses disproportionality metrics. Lay readers need translation without alarmism; editors calibrate verbs.

Genetic testing consumer kits return ancestry and health risk estimates with variable clinical utility. Genetic counselors interpret variance; headlines sometimes oversell certainty.

Global health equity debates cover patent waivers, manufacturing capacity, and cold-chain logistics. COVID-era lessons inform future pandemic planning. Static markdown cannot track evolving policy—refresh deliberately.

Medical AI tools assist imaging reads and documentation; liability frameworks evolve. FDA pathways for software as a medical device differ from general-purpose LLM chatbots. Separate concerns in reporting.

Substance-use disorder treatment blends medication, counseling, and housing support. Stigma reduction language matters. Word choices here aim neutral; lived-experience advocates may prefer stronger person-first framing.
