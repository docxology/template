# William Blake & Bimetallism

**William Blake and Bimetallism: Meta-stability, Macroeconomics, and Nonduality in Poetics and Finance**

This project provides a dense, deeply scholarly 18-chapter manuscript mapping William Blake's anti-materialist cosmology against 500 years of structural macroeconomic history. It leverages the mathematical boundaries of Bimetallism (1558 Elizabethan debasement through the 2026 American Monetary Federalism movement) to frame Blake's "Fourfold Vision" and critique the entropic meta-stability mandated by Gresham's Law.

## Project Scope & Highlights

1. **Macroeconomic History**: Analyzes the structural disparities between Newton's imperial 15.21:1 ratio (1717) and Hamilton's republican 15:1 ratio (1792), identifying the specific mechanical miscalibrations that guaranteed 19th-century transatlantic financial volatility.
2. **Political Theology of Money**: Integrates deep academic orthodoxy (Milton Friedman, Marc Flandreau, Angela Redish) to map the conflict between agrarian silver (tangible labor; Orc) and centralized gold (abstract hierarchy; Urizen). Explores the ultimate culmination of Urizenic abstraction in the 1913–1971 decoupling of currency from the labor theory of value.
3. **Prophetic Inversion**: Details how Blake's illuminated methodologies dissolve dualistic boundaries, providing a philosophical critique of the "Crime of 1873", the "Cross of Gold", and the modern fiat void. 
4. **Programmatic Visualization**: The manuscript is natively supported by a dedicated `src/viz/` subpackage that programmatically generates 6 high-fidelity mathematical charts, including the 3D Topological Fracture of Gresham's Law and Transatlantic Specie Flow bounds.
