# Abstract

This note connects ideal-gas and reference-liquid densities to a reproducible, openly specified **composite mixture model** for arthropod-scale effective density. Numbers reported here are **model outputs** under stated assumptions: the mass-fraction harmonic mixing rule developed in the “Insect composite model” section, tabulated component densities for cuticle, soft tissue, hemolymph, and a phenomenological **internal gas** compartment, and fixed handbook-style fluid anchors [@haynes2014crc]. They are not species-specific measurements.

Under the built-in illustrative presets, central mixture densities fall near **900–1030 kg m⁻³**, i.e. within a few percent of fresh water at 25 °C (997 kg m⁻³ in `fluid_reference`). Ideal dry air at standard temperature and pressure evaluates to about **1.29 kg m⁻³** from `ideal_gas_density_kg_m3`, bracketed by a documented literature band **1.18–1.32 kg m⁻³** for sanity checks—not a fitted uncertainty on the insect model. Sensitivity to the uncertain internal tracheal/air **mass** fraction is summarized as a closed interval by rescaling non-gas compartments while sweeping `internal_gas`; this is a **parameter box**, not a posterior distribution.

The accompanying code in `projects/density_bioscales/src/` drives table exports (`density_summary.json`, `preset_densities.csv`) and figures referenced in Section "Results". Buoyancy labels (`float`, `neutral`, `sink`) are qualitative helpers for comparing model body density to a chosen still fluid. The intended use is methodological: keep assumptions visible, keep outputs byte-reproducible, and separate transparent fluid physics from deliberately coarse biomechanical cartoons.
