# Results

All numeric values below come from `density_summary.json` produced by `01_generate_density_tables.py` unless noted. They are **model artefacts** for the stated presets and component table.

## Reference fluids and presets on one scale

Figure \ref{fig:density_overview} compares illustrative preset mixture densities to reference liquids and ideal dry air at STP. The horizontal dashed line marks **997.0 kg m⁻³** (`WATER_DENSITY_25C_KG_M3`), the fresh-water anchor used in `scenario_water_contrast_at_25c`. Numeric labels at the bar ends report each density to one decimal place for quick cross-check against the JSON export.

**Ideal dry air (STP)** evaluates to about **1.29 kg m⁻³** in the ideal-gas implementation, sitting inside the fixed literature band **1.18–1.32 kg m⁻³**. **Ethanol** at 20 °C (**789 kg m⁻³**) and **fresh water** at 15 °C (**999.1 kg m⁻³**) and 25 °C (**997.0 kg m⁻³**) bracket the insect-scale model outputs. **Seawater** at the illustrative 15 °C anchor (**1025 kg m⁻³**) lies above the 25 °C fresh-water reference, which matters when interpreting buoyancy in marine versus freshwater settings at the level of these scalars.

**Preset central mixture densities** under the default component table are approximately: **adult_fly_illustrative** 913.6 kg m⁻³, **larva_illustrative** 1028.3 kg m⁻³, **arid_beetle_illustrative** 902.8 kg m⁻³. Thus the larva-like split yields the **densest** effective body in this trio (more aqueous tissue, less internal gas mass fraction in the preset), while the beetle-like and fly-like presets cluster lower but still **above** 997 kg m⁻³—so `buoyancy_regime` against 25 °C fresh water reports **sink** for all three central points.

## Internal gas mass fraction: interval envelope

Figure \ref{fig:preset_envelopes} summarizes sensitivity of \(\rho\) to the `internal_gas` mass fraction alone. For each preset, the orange dot is the **nominal** mixture density. The thick horizontal segment spans **`internal_gas_export_sweep_bounds`**: lower bound 0.01, upper bound \(\max(0.02, w_{\mathrm{gas}} + 0.04)\) with \(w_{\mathrm{gas}}\) the preset’s internal gas fraction. Non-gas compartments are **scaled proportionally** so mass fractions sum to unity (`sweep_internal_gas_mass_fraction_interval`). This is a deliberate **parameter box** for exports—not a calibrated uncertainty.

For the current JSON export, approximate sweep intervals (kg m⁻³) are: **adult_fly** about 817–1036 (nominal 914), **larva** about 908–1028 (nominal 1028, upper endpoint coincides with nominal when the sweep hits the high fraction bound), **arid_beetle** about 808–1058 (nominal 903). The larva preset’s narrow nominal gas fraction produces a wide harmonic response when gas mass is allowed to rise toward the upper sweep bound: small changes in \(w_{\mathrm{gas}}\) move \(\rho\) noticeably because the effective gas density (240 kg m⁻³) sits far below tissue anchors.

Vertical references repeat **25 °C fresh water** (dashed) and **15 °C illustrative seawater** (dotted). Intervals that cross the fresh-water line imply that, within this **single-parameter** sweep, neutral buoyancy in fresh water is **compatible** with the model for some admissible \(w_{\mathrm{gas}}\). The nominal orange markers sit above 997 kg m⁻³ for all three presets, yet the **fly** and **beetle** envelopes extend **below** 997 kg m⁻³ at the endpoint with **higher** `internal_gas` mass fraction (the effective gas compartment pulls the harmonic mean toward lower \(\rho\)). The **larva** envelope’s lower bound remains just under 908 kg m⁻³—still below fresh water—so even this “dense” preset intersects the neutral-buoyancy regime somewhere inside the swept box.
