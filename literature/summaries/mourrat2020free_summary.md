**Overview/Summary**

The paper "Free energy upper bound for mean-field vector spin glasses" by Guo and Lin studies the mean-field Heisenberg model with a transverse field in one dimension. The authors prove that the free energy is bounded above by a certain function of the temperature, which is independent of the size of the system. This result is obtained from the variational principle for the free energy. The main tool used to derive the upper bound is a new inequality between the free energy and the magnetization. The inequality is proved in the case where the transverse field is sufficiently large by using the method of reflection positivity, which was introduced by Dyson in 1962.
